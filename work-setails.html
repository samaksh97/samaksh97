Fashion Recommendation system using Graph Neural Networks(GNN)

Georgia Tech Web Search and Graphs Project (CSE 6240), 2023

Numerous industries have benefited from the use of machine learning, and the fashion industry is no exception. By gaining a better understanding of what makes a "good" outfit, companies can provide useful product recommendations to their users. In this project, I follow two approaches that employ graphs to represent outfits and use modified versions of the Graph neural network (GNN) frameworks. Both Node-wise Graph Neural Network (NGNN) and Hypergraph Neural Network (HGNN) aim to score a set of items according to the outfit compatibility of items. The data used is the Polyvore Dataset which consists of curated outfits with product images and text descriptions for each product in an outfit. We compare the two existing models on their performance on two tasks – (1) Fill-in-the-blank (FITB): finding an item that completes an outfit, and (2) Compatibility prediction: estimating compatibility of different items grouped as an outfit. I achieved an improvement of 11% in outfit compatibility prediction tasks over GNN by implementing the Hyper-GNN using multi-modal data I also tried to use embeddings generated from a vision transformer (ViT) and witness enhanced prediction accuracy across the board.

Slow Selling SKU Time Series Forecasting

Best Buy Sales Forecasting Challenge

The challenge of Slow Selling Time Series Forecasting lies in its intricate analytics landscape. Unlike fast-moving products, predicting sales for slow-moving SKUs demands nuanced consideration of various factors. I employed over 5 time series models to unravel the complexities of Best Buy's weekly sales predictions. This intricate task involves integrating demographic, store, holiday features, and mid-scale enterprise data, offering a holistic view of influencing variables. Our predictive arsenal includes ARIMAX models and the Croston Model, each contributing to refining sales projections. Achieving a Weighted Mean Absolute Error (WMAE) of 85%, our approach optimises inventory management and addresses the inherent challenges posed by the unpredictability and sporadic demand patterns of slow-selling SKUs.

Norm Learning with MCMC Sampling

Proceedings of the Thirtieth International Joint Conference on Artificial Intelligence, 2021

There is much interest in techniques that allow agents to represent and reason about social norms that govern agent interactions. Much of this work assumes that norms are provided to agents, but some work has investigated how agents can identify the norms present in a society through observation and experience. However, the norm-identification techniques proposed in the literature often depend on a very specific and domain-specific representation of norms, or require that the possible norms can be enumerated in advance. This paper investigates the problem of identifying norm candidates from a normative language expressed as a probabilistic context-free grammar, using Markov Chain Monte Carlo (MCMC) search.

Portfolio Optimisation with Kernel Search

Bachelors Thesis, VGSOM, IIT KGP, 2019

Index Tracking problem deals with determining a portfolio of assets whose performance replicates, as closely as possible, that of a financial market index (or any arbitrary benchmark chosen), measured by tracking error. Enhanced index tracking improves upon the original problem of Index Tracking by additionally trying to maximise the excess return of the tracking portfolio (over the benchmark) while limiting tracking error, or in other words the optimal portfolio is expected to outperform the benchmark with minimal additional risk over the index.
